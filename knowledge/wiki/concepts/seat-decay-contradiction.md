# The seat-decay contradiction

A pricing-model tension at the heart of the buying decision:

- Contact-center cost is **up to 95% labor** [src-gartner-2022-labor]. Automation's value
  comes from releasing seats.
- But vendors priced **per agent seat** lose revenue exactly as automation succeeds — their
  incentive is misaligned with the customer's goal of shedding seats.
- **Consumption / outcome pricing** resolves this: the customer keeps the savings as seats
  fall, and the vendor is paid for usage/outcomes rather than headcount.

[Kore.ai / ABL](../entities/kore-ai.md) aligns with consumption/outcome pricing, so its
commercial model does not fight the value it creates. This is a qualitative pillar of the
[value thesis](../syntheses/contact-center-ai-value-thesis.md), distinct from the cost-curve
moat.
